// 003 — MANIFESTO // 003 — MANIFIESTO

The images we believe we see do not represent the world but produce us as subjects. With each major transformation in the visual apparatus (perspective, photography, computational image) the way we look has shifted, and with it the interior architecture of the human has been reconstructed, again and again. We find, for instance, that the Renaissance did not discover geometric space but manufactured a new kind of person: sovereign, centered, conquering.

The present moment slips constantly toward another threshold. The computational image has no point of view. The first photograph of a black hole required the entire planet as its instrument (a singular eye? a new sovereign gaze? an Albertian window?) Something new emerges. It can be seen.

Speculative Warfare is above all a theoretical hub operating at this threshold. It thinks at the intersection of media, image, philosophy, and liberation politics. It holds that the question of visuality is inseparable from the question of what kind of subject, and what kind of world, remains possible.

Let this be, then, perhaps an attempt to see clearly at the present moment in which the gaze itself is being reconceived.

Las imágenes que creemos ver no representan el mundo pero nos producen como sujetos. Con cada mayor transformación en el aparato visual (ya sea perspectiva, fotografía o imagen computacional) ha cambiado asimismo cómo miramos y, de esta forma, se ha reconstruido una y otra vez la arquitectura interior del humano. Corroboramos así, por ejemplo, que si es cierto que en el Renacimiento no se descubrió el espacio geométrico, sí se fabricó en cambio un nuevo tipo de persona: soberana, centrada, conquistadora.

El actual presente se escurre hacia otro umbral constantemente; la imagen computacional no tiene punto de vista. La primera fotografía de un agujero negro requirió del planeta entero como instrumento (¿ojo singular, nueva mirada soberana, ventana albertiana?) Algo nuevo emerge, se ve.

Speculative Warfare es ante todo un hub teórico operando en este umbral. Piensa en la intersección de medios, imagen, filosofía y políticas de liberación. Sostiene que la pregunta por la visualidad es inseparable de la pregunta por qué tipo de sujeto, y qué tipo de mundo sigue siendo posible.

Que esto sea, acaso, un intento, entonces, de ver con claridad en el momento presente en que la mirada misma está siendo reconcebida.

// The Founder Aaron Rodríguez Orsi is an Italian-Peruvian researcher in media theory, image, and liberation politics, based in Utrecht, The Netherlands. Trained in humanities at the Pontifical Catholic University of Peru, he has published academic work on memory, political violence, and cultural production in Latin America. His current research traces the relationship between visual paradigm shifts and the production of human subjectivity from Renaissance magic and perspective through photography to the computational image, asking what kind of planetary subject emerges at the current threshold.

He is the founder of Speculative Warfare.
